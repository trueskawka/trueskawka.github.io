RC - day 33

Achievements:

  • architectural whiteboarding
  • mob coding with brownie shots
  • committing the weekend to focus

I was worried the whiteboarding will not go well, as I’ve never done it before and didn’t feel I know enough to answer any questions. I’ve read up on anything I could find on the topic and headed to Turing a bit lightheaded.

The flaw in my reasoning was forgetting that this was RC and the purpose is to learn, not stress. I’ve spent 3 wonderful hours with Bert, Alicia, Ivan and James, going through 5 questions altogether. When we finished I felt a bit dizzy from all the problem solving.

Afterwards, Joe, Cihan and me followed through with the idea of mob coding with enhanced brownies. A few rounds of Malibu with milk later and full of chocolate, we’ve managed to solve a fairly simple graph problem.

I couldn’t stay for the debugging, as today my cat sitting responsibilities started. Both fluffs are keen to play and curious, chasing after anything that moves (even if it doesn’t). Danielle and David’s apartment is full of books on programming and art, with even more fiction, especially of the sf nature. With cats, books and loads of space, it feels like a perfect focused getaway.
