RC - day 41

Achievements:

  • got a lot of rest
  • reflected on my first six weeks at RC
  • discovered my patronus

Six weeks ago I came to NYC with a very vague idea of what I wanted to do specifically. There seemed to be so many things I wanted and ‘had’ to learn - algorithms, networking, CS, ‘proper’ programming (whatever that means), machine learning, better visualisations, APIs, audio processing etc. The list could go on and on, as for the first few days I was utterly distracted and lost.

Over the course of the first half of my batch, I’ve tried and resigned from many things. I didn’t work enough on my ‘big’ project, I felt that some methods of learning or topics I wanted to learn do not resonate with me. I’ve been going to workshops, sometimes a few a day, trying to learn as much as possible. At the same time, I was constantly amazed by the degree of focus of some of my fellow batchers, especially those who came here for ‘only’ six weeks.

The health scare last week was a wake-up call - I’ve been overworking myself, overdosing on the wonderful experience that is RC. Yes, it was great to get up every morning full of plans and with my to-do list bursting. To rush for my morning meditation and check-in, feeling like I’m moving forward. But I need to shift my focus and adjust my goals, otherwise I might lose my motivation altogether. After all, it’s all about never graduating.

As such, I have a few areas and goals to focus on, distilled from the vast universe of possibilities:

  • I still want to build my language-learning app, a project I’ve spread into tiny tasks that are now somewhere near the end of my to-do list under an avalanche of ideas

  • I love talking to people and I’ve been doing a lot of that, but I want to talk more about programming, which means more pairing - at least part of it should be focused on pursuing my projects

  • shortlist of bigger projects:
    • language-learning app (possible pairing on audio processing, APIs and web dev)
    • learning React while building an app (I have some ideas I would like to work on, especially with Ivo)
    • building my own Markdown editor, either web or for MacOS
  • career goals:
    • writing 10 professional blog posts by the end of my stay in the US
    • applying for the remote opportunity I’m excited about (but don’t want to write about it too much, as not to jinx it)
    • having at least 2 interview prep sessions a week
  • productive procrastination:
    • an ongoing list of shorter projects to learn specific programming skills
    • making small 3D printed prototypes for future DYIing

  • 5 hours a week for a non-programming learning project

  • spending Sundays and most of Saturdays on sightseeing/socializing

All in all, the first half of my batch was a perspective-changing experience. I’ll work on the next six weeks to be even better.

Other than reflections, plans and rest, I’ve had a fun few minutes with discovering my patronus on Pottermore. Apparently it’s a weasel, which doesn’t seem too far removed from a squirrel I’ve always imagined it to be.
