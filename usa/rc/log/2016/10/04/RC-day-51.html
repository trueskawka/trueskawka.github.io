RC - day 51

Achievements:

  • read up on Zulip documentation, roadmap and issues
  • had a fruitful conversation with Sumana on Outreachy
  • set up a meeting with Michael on his Zulip feature
  • planned next steps
  • check-out
  • started a course in effective thinking

As I work towards the Outreachy internship, I get more and more comfortable with thinking about the big projects and tackling them in small chunks. I’ve reread a big part of Zulip documentation, studied the roadmap and current issues, trying to figure out where my skills and expectations would fit best.

Sumana is a knowledgable, skillful and amazing person. I’ve spent with her only 1.5 hours today and not only did she help me with my Outreachy ideas and priorities, pointing out some areas my skills could be best used for, but also gave a ton of inspiration on how to grow. I’ve been getting more and more inspired thanks to:

  • Sumana’s blog
  • Tom’s work
  • Moriel’s take on RTL language support

I also think that, given my experience and skills, building features for right-to-left language support and other internationalization aspects of apps sounds like a good project to focus on, while also remembering that I want to work on more than one area, since multiple goals are more likely to help me grow.

Michael, an RC alumnus, build a stream filtering feature for Zulip and I’m meeting with him on Thursday to understand it better and be able to work on it, as to merge it with the codebase. There is also an interesting feature for counting users following a stream and I’ll look into it tomorrow, to see what I’ll need help with.

One of the next steps in the application process is to learn how to use Vagrant properly - at the moment I’m mostly running commands I don’t fully understand, which doesn’t help with troubleshooting.

Second day of check-outs worked well. The group got a little big bigger, though considering the format a 25% increase is pretty substantial and I’d say 5 people is really the limit if it’s going to work as a method for improving accountability. The more people in the group, the less engaged they are and I think engagement is the key. Same as yesterday, others have done so much I’m motivated to do more for tomorrow’s meeting.

Just to have some entertainment that doesn’t feel like wasting much time, I’ve started a course on Effective Thinking through Mathematics. Improving analytical skills is always useful and I need a break from time to time.

Also, the best quote I’ve stumbled upon today, thanks to Rose: knowledge is power - it’s measured in wats.
